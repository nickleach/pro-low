The Story Kevin Aines

In the summer of 2014, I attended a P.G.A. teaching seminar at the Idle Hour Country Club in Macon, Georgia. During the seminar one of the guest speakers had invented a golf swing teaching aid. His presentation from the first idea of the invention until the final product was incredible. The Journey, he said, was the success story. In his final comments he suggested that if you have an idea, “Go for it.” That comment stuck with me even though at that time I didn’t have a thought about any type of invention or product.

Then it happened!

October 2014, after a day at the Club giving a couple of putting lessons, I headed home. Sitting on the back patio listening to Jimmy Buffett and enjoying a Rum cocktail, it came to me, from where I have no clue. I went into the house, picked up a pad and started sketching. As I sketched, the ideas kept coming; an alignment bar with sliding supports, numbers for distance control and a narrow opening support to challenge the player. Then, the name just came out, The Pro Low Putting System. The next morning I am at the hardware store buying plumbing parts to put together a proto type. Next came the PVC proto types. The idea now has a true form, but this was just the start.

My journey has now begun.
